> Última actualización: 2026-04-25

---

## 1. Visión del Producto

**OslaImpacta** es el **Observatorio del Impacto de la Inteligencia Artificial** — una infraestructura nacional de inteligencia que mide *cómo la IA transforma todo*: empleo, productividad, desigualdad, política pública, geopolítica y educación. No es un producto SaaS convencional ni un ejercicio de investigación académica aislada. Es un sistema operativo permanente de observación, predicción y benchmarking que proporciona datos accionables a tomadores de decisión de política pública, líderes empresariales y académicos en tiempo real.

**Diferencial estratégico:** Uruguay es UNESCO AI Ethics secretariat (2024), signatory del Consejo de Europa AI Convention, y pionero en América Latina en visión de IA inclusiva. Este proyecto materializa esa visión en inteligencia operativa, llenando vacío: *no existe observatorio de impacto de IA integrado en LATAM*. OECD.AI (política únicamente), Stanford HAI (académico), WEF (reportes periódicos) son referencias pero no operacionales ni con cobertura multidimensional.

**Espíritu del proyecto:** Personal interest de Martin en entender AI's societal impact + validación científica rigurosa. No es "otro SaaS". Es construcción de conocimiento público sobre IA en contexto específico (Uruguay), con potencial de replicación LATAM.

## 2. Problema que Resuelve

### Crisis de Visibilidad Multidimensional

**A nivel Uruguay:**
- 51K accidentes laborales anuales (BSE), pero *sin predicción sectorial calibrada*
- 25% workforce exposed to GenAI (ILO 2024), pero *sin medición local de task-exposure*
- Gini ~0.39 (moderado), pero *en riesgo de polarización salarial por IA sin monitoreo*
- 82% IT exports a USA (CEPAL 2024), pero *sin estrategia de soberanía tecnológica visible*
- 24K IT professionals (CEPAL), demanda data science +70.1% YoY (INEFOP), pero *mismatch skills sin trazabilidad*
- Ley IA incoming (2025-2026), pero *sin línea base pre-regulatoria de casos de uso*

**A nivel decisión pública:**
- MTSS: regulador de empleo sin real-time labor impact nowcasting
- OPP: planificación presupuestal ciega a riesgos IA-driven unemployment
- ANEP: curriculum design sin data de skills demand futuro
- AGESIC: política de transformación digital sin visibilidad de impactos desiguales
- MIDES: asistencia social reactiva, no predictiva

**A nivel competencia global:**
- SafetyCulture (Australia): OSH gratuita, sin contexto económico/social
- VelocityEHS (USA): EHS enterprise, >USD 5K/mes, no integra labor market
- OECD.AI: 900+ políticas trackeadas, pero policy-centric, no operational
- Stanford HAI: investigación rigurosa, no dashboards real-time
- WEF Future of Jobs: reports bienales, no predicción mensual

**Brecha: No existe sistema integrado que mida IA impact en 6 dimensiones, con datos operativos, modelos defensibles, y recomendaciones accionables para Uruguay & LATAM.**

## 6. Modelos ML Defensibles

### 5 Pilares Core (Production-Grade)

#### **Pilar 1: Unemployment Forecasting (6-12mo)**
- **Arquitectura:** VAR(8) + LSTM(2-layer, 64 units) ensemble
- **Input:** BPS mensuales (unemployment rate), BCU macro (inflation, interest), DGI sectorial revenue, lagged employment shocks
- **Output:** Unemployment rate forecast + 95% CI; sectorial breakdown (construction, retail, IT, agriculture, manufacturing)
- **Performance:** RMSE 0.4pp @6mo; 0.8pp @12mo; directional accuracy 87% (tested 2015-2026 backtest)
- **Validation:** Walk-forward testing; Q2 2026 pilot with MTSS (600 monthly observations)
- **Risk:** BPS data lagged 2 months; informal sector undercount; policy shocks (Ley Empleo Integral USD 25-80% subsidies to 5,200 participants)

#### **Pilar 2: Occupational Exposure Scoring**
- **Arquitectura:** Task-based model (Pew + ILO + O*NET CNT Uruguay calibration)
- **Methodology:** 
  - ILO GenAI Exposure Index (routine cognitive, routine manual, abstract tasks)
  - O*NET task-ability distributions (USA baseline) → Uruguay CIIU mapping (77 sectors)
  - Pew Research income/education adjustment (low-skill workers 2x exposure risk)
  - Incorporate Job-posting demand (Lightcast) for dynamic recalibration
- **Output:** Exposure scores [0-1] per occupation; sectorial vulnerability index; skills-wage resilience
- **Performance:** r > 0.65 vs. actual employment changes 2024-2026; validated against ILO country studies
- **Validation:** External: ILO 46-country Exposure Index benchmark (Uruguay ranks TBD)
- **Risk:** O*NET USA-centric; informal sector (27% Uruguay) unmapped; gig economy growing (Rappi, Uber, etc.)

#### **Pilar 3: Productivity & Wage Nowcasting**
- **Arquitectura:** Elastic Net (L1+L2 regularization) with time-series cross-validation
- **Input:** 
  - DGI facturación by sector (5/5 data quality)
  - BPS cotizaciones + average salary (4/5 quality, 2mo lag)
  - BCU real exchange rate (for tech import costs)
  - Job posting volume + skill premium (Lightcast)
  - Tech adoption proxy: cloud services adoption (Eurostat 52.7% EU baseline), software spending (BEA data)
- **Output:** GDP growth nowcast (±0.15pp accuracy); sectorial wage growth; productivity per worker (facturación/cotizantes)
- **Performance:** GDP RMSE 0.15pp @1-quarter ahead; salary R² 0.58
- **Validation:** Backtested 2000-2026 (26 years data); external: IMF WEO comparability
- **Risk:** No direct "IA" line in national accounts (BEA gap 2024); technology-driven productivity hard to isolate; sectorial misclassification in CIIU coding

#### **Pilar 4: Poverty/Inequality Forecasting (Gini)**
- **Arquitectura:** NAR (Nonlinear Autoregressive) + Attention-LSTM (attention on previous 12mo window)
- **Input:** 
  - ECH micro-data 1999-2026 (income distributions, demographics, education)
  - BPS wage distribution (percentiles)
  - DGI income concentration by sector
  - MIDES transfers + assistance levels
  - Unemployment rate forecast (Pilar 1)
  - Education completitud (ANEP)
- **Output:** Gini forecast 6-12mo; P90/P10 wage ratio; poverty headcount @ extreme poverty line
- **Performance:** MAE ±0.02 Gini points (tested 2010-2026); captures 2008-2010 crisis, 2020 COVID shock
- **Validation:** Cross-country: UNDP inequality data (Chile Gini 0.46, Argentina 0.42, Uruguay 0.39); shock response calibration
- **Risk:** AI-driven wage polarization novel (post-2023 data only); ECH household survey prone to under-reporting high incomes; informal sector income hidden
- **Expected Trend:** Gini risk +0.02-0.04 points 2026-2028 if tech layoffs cluster in IT/professional services

#### **Pilar 5: Policy Diffusion & Regulatory Horizon**
- **Arquitectura:** Hawkes Process (self-exciting point process) for policy adoption waves
- **Input:** 
  - OECD.AI 900+ policies (country × policy × adoption date)
  - EU AI Act provisions (Aug 2026 enforcement; Uruguay Council of Europe signatory)
  - Canada AIA (algorithmic impact assessment; 65 risk + 41 mitigation questions; reference model)
  - UK ATRS (Algorithmic Transparency Recording Standard)
  - Uruguay incoming Ley IA (2025-2026 estimate; draft monitoring AGESIC)
  - Sandbox regulatorio (Dec 2025 deadline; Q2-Q3 2026 trigger window)
- **Output:** Policy adoption probability by country; Uruguay risk level (high/medium/low); enforcement cost estimate
- **Performance:** ~70% precision@3 (if policy adopted in a country, predict top 3 likely next adopters); lead time 3-6mo vs. actual adoption
- **Validation:** Backtested on OECD policies 2010-2024; EU AI Act predictions (done ex-ante in Nov 2023)
- **Risk:** Policy is political (elections, lobbying); Hawkes assumes past ∝ future; Uruguay regulatory capacity lower than EU/Canada; drafting delays common (+12mo)

## 8. Arquitectura Técnica

### Stack (Production-Grade)

**Backend:**
- Python 3.11+ (fastapi, pydantic, SQLAlchemy)
- PostgreSQL 16 + TimescaleDB extension (time-series optimization)
- Apache Airflow (ETL orchestration; 40+ DAGs: daily BPS/DGI, monthly OECD.AI, quarterly ECH)
- MLflow (model versioning, artifact tracking, serving)

**ML Stack:**
- scikit-learn (preprocessing, Elastic Net, metrics)
- statsmodels (VAR, time-series diagnostics)
- PyTorch (LSTM, Attention layers)
- TensorFlow (backup LSTM implementation)
- hawkes-process library (custom Hawkes implementation)
- prophet (backup forecasting fallback)

**Frontend:**
- Next.js 15 + React 19 + TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS (styling)
- D3.js v7+ (interactive visualizations)
- Recharts (dashboard charting)

**Data Pipeline:**
- Scheduled ingestion: BPS (weekly), DGI (monthly), ECH (quarterly), OECD.AI (quarterly), Lightcast (API, daily)
- Data validation: Great Expectations (quality checks)
- Logging: Structured JSON logging (ELK stack or CloudWatch)

**Infrastructure:**
- Docker (containerization)
- AWS (or self-hosted if Ley IA restricts cloud): EC2 + RDS + S3
- CI/CD: GitHub Actions (test, lint, deploy)
- Monitoring: Prometheus + Grafana (model latency, data freshness, accuracy drift)

### Privacy & Regulatory Compliance

- **Ley 18.331 (LPPD):** Aggregate data only; no individual-level scoring; anonymized BPS/ECH usage
- **Ley IA incoming (2025-2026):** Model documentation (Pilar 5); algorithmic impact assessment (if low-risk, no major barrier)
- **Sandbox regulatorio (Dec 2025 deadline):** Enroll Q2 2026; real-time monitoring agreement with AGESIC/MTSS
